Current Venture

Operational AI that executes the work humans usually do between tools.

2025–Now · Operational AI

Efficidex

Every team uses 10–30 tools, but most of the real work still happens between them: data entry, sanity checks, routing, follow-ups, reconciliations. That’s the actual operational drag.

Efficidex runs these workflows automatically using autonomous agents that work inside your existing stack.

Agents today handle:

  • Invoice → approval → payment → reconciliation loops.
  • Lead intake, qualification, and CRM hygiene.
  • Support ticket triage, resolution workflows, and updates.
  • HR onboarding and recurring internal operations.

No migration. No “copilot”. No bots clicking UI. Just real workflows executed where your data already lives.

Thesis

Dashboards don’t run companies. Systems do.

People repeat the same workflows every day — copying, checking, updating, fixing, routing. That shouldn’t belong to headcount. It should belong to systems.

Principles I build with:

  • Workflows > tasks — automate the whole loop, not isolated clicks.
  • Remove operational drag before adding people.
  • Learn from logs and real-world execution, not assumptions.
  • Reliability beats cleverness in production systems.
  • Simplicity scales; complexity collapses under real usage.

Everything I build moves work from manual → automatic → autonomous.

About

I skipped the degree path and learned by shipping systems that had to work.

Builder first, self-taught

After 12th, I didn’t go down the standard degree route. Instead, I spent my time building systems that either worked in the real world — or broke in the real world. Robots in public environments, commerce experiments, retail tech, and now operational AI.

My process is simple: ship → break → learn → fix → scale. That’s where I learned engineering, UX, operations, customer psychology, and speed.

I care more about whether a system holds up under real usage than how it looks in a deck. Clarity, reliability and shipped outcomes matter more than theatrics.
